About James A. Garfield

James Abram Garfield (November 19, 1831 â€“ September 19, 1881) was the 20th president of the United States. He served for a mere 199 days before being assassinated. Garfield was a Civil War hero, a U.S. Congressman, and an advocate for civil service reform.

Garfield was shot by Charles J. Guiteau on July 2, 1881, and despite efforts to save him, Garfield died from infections related to the wound, highlighting the medical inadequacies of the time.

Timeline of James A. Garfield's Life

  • November 19, 1831
    James Abram Garfield is born in Orange, Ohio.
  • 1851-1854
    Attends Williams College in Massachusetts.
  • 1861-1863
    Serves as a Major General in the Union Army during the Civil War, notable for his leadership in the Battle of Middle Creek.
  • 1863
    Garfield is elected to the U.S. House of Representatives, where he serves for 17 years.
  • 1880
    Garfield is elected as the 20th President of the United States after a contentious Republican convention.
  • March 4, 1881
    Garfield is inaugurated as President of the United States.
  • May 19, 1881
    Garfield delivers a speech to the Republican National Committee, emphasizing civil service reform.
  • July 2, 1881
    Garfield is shot by Charles J. Guiteau, a disgruntled office seeker, at the Washington, D.C. railroad station.
  • September 19, 1881
    James A. Garfield dies from his wounds after a prolonged illness. He is succeeded by Vice President Chester A. Arthur.
  • October 1881
    Garfield is remembered and mourned as a martyr for civil service reform, as his death fuels efforts to pass the Pendleton Civil Service Reform Act.
